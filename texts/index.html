Gnostic Texts & Gospels

A comprehensive collection of primary Gnostic texts and gospels discovered at Nag Hammadi and other archaeological sites. These writings represent the diverse theological perspectives of early Gnostic Christianity.

The texts presented here are among the most significant primary sources for understanding Gnostic Christianity. Discovered in 1945 near Nag Hammadi, Egypt, these manuscripts provide invaluable insight into the beliefs, practices, and interpretations of Jesus's teachings held by Gnostic communities in the early centuries of the Common Era.

Each text is presented with scholarly context, historical background, and key passages highlighted for deeper exploration.

Primary Texts

Gospel of Thomas

Sayings Gospel

A collection of 114 sayings attributed to Jesus without narrative framework. This text represents an early layer of Jesus tradition and emphasizes mystical knowledge (gnosis) over salvation history.

  • Date: 1st century (possibly pre-70 CE)
  • Discovery: Nag Hammadi Library, 1945
  • Language: Coptic (translated from Greek)

Gospel of Philip

Wisdom Teaching

A collection of mystical sayings and teachings emphasizing the nature of gnosis, resurrection, and the relationship between Christ and the soul. Explores symbolic interpretations of Christian sacraments.

  • Date: 2nd century CE
  • Discovery: Nag Hammadi Library, 1945
  • Language: Coptic (translated from Greek)
Read Full Text →

Gospel of Mary Magdalene

Revelation Dialogue

A dialogue in which Mary Magdalene receives revelations from the risen Jesus and transmits them to the apostles. Emphasizes the spiritual vision necessary for salvation and Mary's role as a beloved disciple.

  • Date: 2nd century CE
  • Discovery: Berlin Codex and Nag Hammadi fragments
  • Language: Coptic
Read Full Text →

Apocryphon of John

Revelation Treatise

A foundational Gnostic text presenting a complete cosmological system including the nature of the true God, the Demiurge, and the path of salvation through gnosis. One of the most comprehensive Gnostic cosmologies.

  • Date: 2nd century CE
  • Discovery: Multiple copies in Nag Hammadi Library
  • Language: Coptic
Read Full Text →

Hypostasis of the Archons

Cosmological Treatise

An exposition of Gnostic cosmology describing the nature of the Archons (false rulers), the creation of the material world, and humanity's divine spark trapped within matter. Based on Genesis interpretation.

  • Date: 2nd-3rd century CE
  • Discovery: Nag Hammadi Library, 1945
  • Language: Coptic
Read Full Text →

Thunder, Perfect Mind

Poetic Revelation

A powerful poetic text in which a female divine figure (possibly Sophia or the Holy Spirit) reveals herself through paradoxical statements. Emphasizes the transcendent nature of divine knowledge and feminine wisdom.

  • Date: 2nd century CE
  • Discovery: Nag Hammadi Library, 1945
  • Language: Coptic

About These Texts

The texts in this collection represent the primary sources of Gnostic Christianity. They were composed between the 1st and 3rd centuries CE and reflect the theological developments of early Christian Gnostic communities. Most were suppressed by the orthodox church and survived only through rediscovery in archaeological excavations.

All translations presented here are based on scholarly editions and are provided for educational purposes. For academic study, consultation of peer-reviewed scholarly editions is recommended.
